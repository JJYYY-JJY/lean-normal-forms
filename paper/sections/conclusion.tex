\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We described a completed \Lean{} development of executable row-style \HNF{} over
Euclidean domains. The formalization is centered on three ideas: explicit
proof-facing left certificates, a recursive kernel built from lifted Bezout and
diagonal block gadgets, and a narrow \code{CanonicalMod} boundary that upgrades
computational remainders into exact uniqueness statements. The resulting public
API provides executable HNF, unimodular left certificates, correctness, and
uniqueness. It does not yet provide executable \SNF{} or a PID bridge, and the
paper has been written accordingly.

From an engineering perspective, the main lesson is that canonical algebraic
statements in \Lean{} benefit from separating three concerns that are often
entangled: replay semantics for elementary operations, matrix-level certificate
composition, and exact canonical-remainder reasoning. The next stage is to reuse
the same architecture for Smith normal form and then connect the resulting
invariant factors to \mathlib{}'s abstract PID theory.
