\section{Conclusion}

Strong Smith certificates with explicit inverses turn the textbook homology
algorithm into a compositional formal construction.  The first Smith result
provides an actual integral kernel basis, the chain condition proves that
the incoming map lies in that kernel, and the second result classifies the
resulting cokernel.  The final equivalence concerns the intrinsic homology
quotient, while a proved projection keeps the full matrix path executable.

The v1.2.2 application remains separate from the stable normal-form facade
and has its own API freeze, native test, audit, paper, and artifact.  Its
comparison with mathlib categorical homology handles the degree-zero
short-complex distinction explicitly.  For simplicial input, a certified
realization witness supplies exactly the additional identities and basis
compatibility needed to transport the decomposition to normalized chains and
the normalized Moore complex; no reconstruction theorem from weaker face
data is assumed.
