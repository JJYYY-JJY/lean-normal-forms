\section{Conclusion}

Strong polynomial Smith normal form provides a compact interface between
execution and the algebraic proof of rational canonical form.  The same
normalized diagonal drives the cyclic decomposition, characteristic and
minimal polynomial identities, companion blocks, and similarity invariants.
Recording explicit inverses makes the final statement directly usable by
later certified linear-algebra applications.

The implementation deliberately leaves the stable HNF/SNF facade unchanged.
Users opt into the rational-canonical application through its own import,
paper, benchmark, release gate, and artifact.  This separation permits later
homology and faster-algorithm work to depend on the same strong core without
coupling their release claims.
